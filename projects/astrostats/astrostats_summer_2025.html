GP-based Imputation in
Kepler Stellar Light Curves

AstroStatistics Group, Penn State University · First authored paper

↓ Download Paper (PDF) GitHub — Simulations GitHub — Research Repo (Private)

Overview

This project was completed during the AstroStatistics summer research programme at Penn State University in 2025. The work develops a Gaussian Process framework for imputing missing observations in Kepler stellar photometric time series.

Kepler light curves are affected by instrumental gaps, safe-mode events, and scheduled downtime that result in missing data. Classical interpolation approaches fail to respect the correlated, non-stationary nature of stellar flux. This study applies GP regression with kernels tuned to stellar variability timescales.

Methods

Three stellar variability classes are studied: quiet solar-like stars, RR Lyrae pulsators, and short-period eclipsing binaries. For each class, appropriate covariance kernels are selected (squared-exponential, quasi-periodic, and composite kernels respectively) and hyperparameters are fitted by marginal likelihood maximisation.

The GP imputation procedure produces posterior predictive distributions over missing segments, enabling principled uncertainty quantification rather than point-fill imputation.

Highlights

  • First application of class-specific GP kernels to Kepler gap-filling
  • Posterior predictive coverage analysis across three variability types
  • Comparison against linear interpolation and LOWESS baselines
  • Implementation in R using the kernlab and gplite packages
